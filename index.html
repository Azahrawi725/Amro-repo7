About me

I’m a PhD candidate in Civil Engineering at LSU with the WISE research team, focusing on wind engineering and renewable-energy structures. My work studies wind-induced torsional galloping in single-axis solar trackers using high-fidelity CFD and large-scale experiments to define stability limits and develop practical mitigation strategies.

I develop and validate OpenFOAM simulations on Linux (dynamic mesh/ALE, turbulence, ABL inflow) to predict loads and aeroelastic response. In parallel, I run instrumented wind-tunnel tests and turn measurements into design-focused stability maps and metrics. I also use ML/AI surrogates to speed up instability prediction and improve tracker reliability for utility-scale agrivoltaics.

    Under Review / Submitted

    • Aeroelastic Characterization of Solar Trackers Torsional Galloping Through CFD Modeling in OpenFOAM (Submitted)
      Develops and validates an OpenFOAM v24.06 CFD framework (ALE + WALE LES) to predict torsional galloping onset for a 2D single-axis tracker section (Port Allen, LA reference). Presents stability diagrams vs tilt, and quantifies mitigation via stiffness, damping (ζ = 0.1–0.25), and aerodynamic edge modifications—showing up to 81.8% delay in instability and >60% amplitude reduction when combined.

    Draft Papers / Ready to Submit

    • Wind-Induced Torsional Galloping of Solar Panels: Large-Scale Experimental Tests and Mitigation Strategies
      Reports large-scale experimental testing of wind-induced torsional galloping with synchronized sensing (e.g., accelerometers/video tracking) to quantify onset, amplitudes, and damping behavior. Evaluates practical mitigation strategies and provides design-ready recommendations for safer, more reliable PV structures under extreme wind.
    • Realistic ABL Turbulence Inflow Using a Vortex Method for CFD Prediction of Solar Tracker Galloping and Control Strategies
      Introduces a vortex-method turbulence inflow for realistic ABL profiles in CFD, targeting reduced turbulence decay and improved agreement with wind-tunnel/lab profiles. Demonstrates how improved inflow fidelity strengthens galloping prediction and mitigation assessment for tracker configurations.
    • Developing an AI-Based Predictive Model for Solar Tracker Stability with a Comparison with a SimScale Model
      Builds an AI-driven stability predictor trained on CFD/experimental features to rapidly estimate galloping risk across tilt, wind speed, and structural parameters. Benchmarks predictions against a SimScale-based model to compare accuracy, generalization, and practical workflow speed for engineering use.
    • Design And Implementation of An Optimization Platform for Common Fixed Photovoltaic Systems
      Develops an optimization platform to improve fixed PV system performance and reliability by coupling design constraints with parametric evaluation and decision metrics. Provides an end-to-end workflow for screening configurations and guiding design choices with repeatable, engineering-friendly outputs.
